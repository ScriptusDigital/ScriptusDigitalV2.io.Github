Guitar Modes Explained
“Unlock the fretboard — understand the modes, rule the solos.”

Below is a quick reference guide to the seven traditional guitar modes — each with its unique sound, flavor, and emotion.

Mode Scale Formula Character Example Song
Ionian 1 – 2 – 3 – 4 – 5 – 6 – 7 Bright, happy (Major) "Don’t Stop Believin’" – Journey
Dorian 1 – 2 – ♭3 – 4 – 5 – 6 – ♭7 Funky, smooth minor "Oye Como Va" – Santana
Phrygian 1 – ♭2 – ♭3 – 4 – 5 – ♭6 – ♭7 Dark, exotic "Wherever I May Roam" – Metallica
Lydian 1 – 2 – 3 – ♯4 – 5 – 6 – 7 Dreamy, open "Flying in a Blue Dream" – Joe Satriani
Mixolydian 1 – 2 – 3 – 4 – 5 – 6 – ♭7 Bluesy, rock "Sweet Home Alabama" – Lynyrd Skynyrd
Aeolian 1 – 2 – ♭3 – 4 – 5 – ♭6 – ♭7 Melancholic, emotional "Nothing Else Matters" – Metallica
Locrian 1 – ♭2 – ♭3 – 4 – ♭5 – ♭6 – ♭7 Unstable, dissonant "YYZ" – Rush (intro riff hints)

Once you master these, you’ll start hearing modes everywhere — and your solos will sound like they came straight out of an interdimensional arcade.
